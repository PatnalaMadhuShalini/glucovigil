GlucoVigile Health Analytics

Requirements Elicitation and Analysis

1. Introduction to Requirements Elicitation

The requirements elicitation process for GlucoVigile Health Analytics involved a comprehensive approach to gather, analyze, and document the needs of potential users, healthcare providers, and other stakeholders. This document outlines the methods used, findings, and resulting requirements that form the foundation of the system design.

Key Objectives of Requirements Elicitation:

  • Identify the primary user needs for diabetes risk assessment
  • Understand the limitations of existing systems
  • Determine essential features for a comprehensive health analytics platform
  • Establish clear priorities for development
  • Define measurable success criteria

2. Elicitation Methodologies

2.1 Stakeholder Interviews

In-depth interviews were conducted with the following stakeholder groups:

  • Potential End Users (n=24): Individuals of various ages, ethnicities, and health statuses
  • Healthcare Providers (n=8): Primary care physicians, endocrinologists, and diabetes educators
  • Health Technology Experts (n=5): UX designers and health informatics specialists
  • Public Health Officials (n=3): Representatives from health departments

2.2 Competitive Analysis

Analysis of 12 existing diabetes risk assessment tools and health platforms, including:

  • Web-based risk calculators (4)
  • Mobile health applications (5)
  • Clinical screening tools (3)

2.3 User Surveys

Online surveys were distributed to gather quantitative data on user preferences and behaviors:

  • General population survey (n=142)
  • Pre-diabetic individuals survey (n=48)
  • Healthcare provider survey (n=22)

2.4 Focus Groups

Three focus group sessions were conducted with:

  • Health-conscious individuals (8 participants)
  • People with family history of diabetes (6 participants)
  • Healthcare providers and health coaches (5 participants)

2.5 Literature Review

Comprehensive review of:

  • 42 academic papers on diabetes risk factors and prediction models
  • 12 clinical guidelines from medical associations
  • 8 health technology usability studies
  • 5 reports on digital health adoption trends

3. Analysis of Existing Systems

3.1 Current State Assessment

Current diabetes risk assessment systems typically exhibit the following characteristics:

Finding 1: Paper-based questionnaires remain the predominant method for diabetes risk screening in clinical settings, with limited digital integration.
Finding 2: Existing digital tools primarily focus on physiological metrics (BMI, blood pressure, glucose levels) with insufficient consideration of lifestyle and mental health factors.
Finding 3: Most systems provide one-time risk assessments without capabilities for longitudinal tracking or adaptive recommendations.
Finding 4: User interfaces of existing tools often employ medical terminology without adequate explanation, creating barriers for users with limited health literacy.
Finding 5: Integration with external health data sources (EHRs, wearables) is minimal or non-existent in most consumer-facing applications.

3.2 Limitations of Current Approaches

Limitation Impact on Users Opportunity for Improvement
Generic risk predictions Limited personal relevance, reduced motivation to act Personalized, granular risk assessment algorithms
Siloed health data Incomplete risk picture, burden of manual data entry Unified data collection with secure import capabilities
Limited actionability Uncertainty about next steps after risk assessment Targeted, prioritized recommendations with progress tracking
Poor user engagement Low retention, inconsistent monitoring Engaging design with meaningful progress visualization
Clinical-focused language Confusion and misinterpretation of results Plain language with embedded educational content
Access barriers Limited reach to underserved populations Multi-platform, accessible design with offline capabilities

4. User Needs and Requirements

4.1 Identified User Needs

End Users:
Need 1 (High Priority): Understand personal diabetes risk without requiring extensive medical knowledge
Need 2 (High Priority): Receive clear, actionable recommendations specific to individual health status
Need 3 (Medium Priority): Track health progress over time with meaningful visualizations
Need 4 (Medium Priority): Understand how specific lifestyle factors impact diabetes risk
Need 5 (Low Priority): Share health reports with healthcare providers
Healthcare Providers:
Need 1 (High Priority): Access comprehensive patient risk profiles based on evidence-based algorithms
Need 2 (Medium Priority): Review patient progress between appointments
Need 3 (Low Priority): Integrate patient-generated data with clinical systems

4.2 Functional Requirements

User Authentication and Profile Management:
FR-1: The system shall provide secure user registration and login functionality
FR-2: The system shall allow users to create and update their health profile
FR-3: The system shall implement privacy controls for sensitive health data
Health Data Input and Management:
FR-4: The system shall enable manual entry of physiological measurements (blood sugar, blood pressure, height, weight)
FR-5: The system shall support input of lifestyle factors (exercise habits, diet quality, work style)
FR-6: The system shall assess mental health and stress levels
FR-7: The system shall allow documentation of family health history
Risk Assessment and Analysis:
FR-8: The system shall calculate a diabetes risk score based on comprehensive health data
FR-9: The system shall categorize risk level (low, moderate, high)
FR-10: The system shall identify and prioritize specific risk factors
FR-11: The system shall visualize risk assessment results in an understandable format
Recommendation Generation:
FR-12: The system shall create personalized health recommendations based on identified risk factors
FR-13: The system shall prioritize high-impact recommendations
FR-14: The system shall adapt recommendations based on user feedback and progress
Health Report Generation:
FR-15: The system shall generate downloadable PDF health assessment reports
FR-16: The system shall provide a summary of key health metrics and risk factors
FR-17: The system shall include recommendations for healthcare providers in the report
Medical Record Processing:
FR-18: The system shall support upload and processing of medical documents
FR-19: The system shall extract relevant health data from uploaded documents
FR-20: The system shall integrate extracted data into the risk assessment

4.3 Non-Functional Requirements

Performance:
NFR-1: The system shall maintain response times under 2 seconds for risk calculations
NFR-2: The system shall support at least 1,000 concurrent users without performance degradation
NFR-3: The system shall process uploaded medical documents within 30 seconds
Security and Privacy:
NFR-4: The system shall comply with HIPAA regulations for data storage and processing
NFR-5: The system shall implement end-to-end encryption for sensitive health information
NFR-6: The system shall provide secure authentication mechanisms including MFA
NFR-7: The system shall give users control over data sharing and retention
Usability:
NFR-8: The system shall provide an intuitive user interface accessible to diverse user groups
NFR-9: The system shall implement mobile and desktop responsive design
NFR-10: The system shall present clear visualization of complex health data
NFR-11: The system shall require minimal steps for core functionality

5. Use Case Scenarios

5.1 Core Use Cases

Use Case 1: New User Registration and Initial Assessment

Primary Actor: End User

Preconditions: User has access to the application

Main Flow:

  1. User accesses the registration page
  2. User provides authentication information (email, password)
  3. System validates user input
  4. User completes demographic information
  5. User enters physiological measurements
  6. User provides lifestyle information
  7. User answers mental health assessment questions
  8. System calculates initial risk assessment
  9. System generates personalized recommendations
  10. System displays dashboard with results

Alternative Flows:

  • If user does not have all health measurements, system allows partial completion with estimated risk
  • If validation fails, system provides specific error messages

Postconditions: User account created, initial risk assessment completed

Use Case 2: Updating Health Data and Reassessment

Primary Actor: End User

Preconditions: User has registered account

Main Flow:

  1. User logs into their account
  2. User navigates to health data section
  3. User enters new physiological measurements
  4. User updates lifestyle information
  5. System automatically recalculates risk score
  6. System compares new results with previous assessment
  7. System adjusts recommendations based on changes
  8. System displays updated dashboard with progress indicators

Postconditions: Updated risk assessment and recommendations

Use Case 3: Generating and Sharing Health Report

Primary Actor: End User

Secondary Actor: Healthcare Provider

Preconditions: User has completed risk assessment

Main Flow:

  1. User navigates to reports section
  2. User selects report generation option
  3. System compiles comprehensive health report
  4. System generates PDF document
  5. User downloads report
  6. User shares report with healthcare provider

Postconditions: Health report generated and available for sharing

6. Requirements Validation

6.1 Validation Methods

The following methods were used to validate the identified requirements:

  • Stakeholder reviews with end users and healthcare providers
  • Prototype testing with target user groups
  • Expert reviews by health informatics specialists
  • Requirement traceability analysis
  • Consistency checking across requirement sets

6.2 Key Validation Findings

The validation process confirmed that the requirements accurately reflect user needs, with the following insights:

  • End users emphasized the importance of clear explanations for medical terminology
  • Healthcare providers validated the clinical relevance of the risk assessment approach
  • Usability testing revealed the need for simpler navigation flows for older users
  • Security requirements were enhanced based on expert feedback
  • Performance requirements were adjusted to accommodate typical user internet connections

7. Requirements Prioritization

7.1 Prioritization Criteria

Requirements were prioritized based on the following criteria:

  • User value: Impact on core user needs and goals
  • Technical feasibility: Complexity and technical risk
  • Business value: Alignment with project objectives
  • Regulatory necessity: Compliance requirements
  • Dependency relationships: Prerequisite capabilities

7.2 Development Phases

Based on prioritization, requirements were allocated to the following development phases:

Phase 1 (MVP):
  • Core user authentication and profile management
  • Basic health data input (physiological, lifestyle)
  • Fundamental risk assessment algorithm
  • Essential security and privacy controls
  • Basic dashboard visualization
Phase 2:
  • Enhanced data input (mental health, family history)
  • Refined risk assessment algorithm
  • Personalized recommendation engine
  • Health report generation
  • Progress tracking visualization
Phase 3:
  • Medical record processing and integration
  • Advanced recommendation adaptation
  • Healthcare provider interface
  • Enhanced security features (MFA, etc.)
  • Performance optimizations

8. Conclusion

The requirements elicitation and analysis process for GlucoVigile Health Analytics has identified a comprehensive set of user needs and system requirements. The findings highlight the importance of creating a personalized, accessible, and actionable platform for diabetes risk assessment that overcomes the limitations of existing approaches.

Key insights from this process include:

  • The critical importance of integrating physical, mental, and lifestyle factors for comprehensive risk assessment
  • The need for clear, jargon-free explanations of health concepts to improve user understanding
  • The value of longitudinal tracking and adaptive recommendations based on user progress
  • The balance between detailed health data collection and streamlined user experience
  • The necessity of robust security measures that don't impede usability

These requirements form the foundation for the GlucoVigile system design and development process, ensuring that the final platform effectively addresses real user needs while overcoming the limitations of current diabetes risk assessment approaches.
